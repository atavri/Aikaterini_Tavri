About Me

I specialize in microwave remote sensing of Arctic sea ice, focusing on synthetic aperture radar (SAR) analysis during the melt season. I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Brown University.

Research Highlights

  • Microwave remote sensing of Arctic sea ice during advanced melt stages.
  • Polarimetric SAR scattering analysis and sea ice type classification.
  • Field validation campaigns integrating radar and optical data.

Expeditions and Fieldwork

In 2020, I participated in the historic MOSAiC Expedition, conducting extensive in-situ measurements of sea ice and snowpack properties aboard the research icebreaker Polarstern. My work focused on integrating field data with microwave remote sensing observations to study Arctic summer sea ice processes.

Selected Publications

  • Macfarlane, A.R., Schneebeli, M., Dadic, R., Tavri, A., et al. (2023). Database of Snow on Sea Ice from MOSAiC. Scientific Data.
